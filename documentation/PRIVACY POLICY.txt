## 1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide Directly

Account Information:
- Email address (required)
- Password (encrypted)
- Display name (optional)
- Profile photo (optional)

Fitness and Health Data (voluntarily provided by you):
- Workout details (exercises, sets, reps, weight, duration)
- Habit tracking (daily check-ins, custom habits)
- Mood and energy levels (1-5 ratings, context tags)
- Nutrition logs (meal ratings, photos, optional calorie/macro data)
- Sleep logs (hours, quality ratings)
- Progress photos
- Body measurements (if you choose to log them)
- Goals and targets
- Free-text notes and comments

Payment Information:
- Payment is processed by Apple (App Store) or Google (Google Play)
- We do not store your credit card or payment details
- We receive transaction confirmations only (subscription status, purchase date)

1.2 Information from Third-Party Integrations

Wearable Devices and Health Platforms (with your explicit permission):
- Apple Health (iOS): Steps, sleep, workouts, heart rate, HRV, active calories
- Google Fit (Android): Steps, sleep, workouts, heart rate

You control which data types we access through device permissions.

1.3 Information Collected Automatically

Usage Data:
- Device information (model, operating system, app version)
- IP address (for security and fraud prevention)
- Log data (timestamps, errors, crashes)
- Analytics events (features used, buttons clicked, screens viewed)

We use analytics to improve the app, not for advertising.

1.4 Information We Do Not Collect

We do not collect:
- Precise geolocation unless explicitly needed for a feature you enable
- Access to your contacts, camera, or microphone (except when you take photos)
- Social media data beyond basic profile info for Apple/Google sign-in

## 2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 To Provide the Service
We use your data to create and manage your account, store your fitness and
wellness data, display your logged information, sync data with wearables,
calculate metrics, and provide insights.

2.2 To Improve the Service
We use anonymized, aggregated data to analyze usage, identify bugs, improve
performance, and develop new features.

2.3 To Communicate with You
We use your email to send account notifications, service updates, and respond
to support requests. Marketing emails are optional and can be opted out.

2.4 For Security and Legal Compliance
We use data to detect and prevent fraud, enforce our Terms, and comply with
legal obligations.

2.5 With Your Consent
For any use not described above, we will request explicit consent.

## 3. How We Share Your Information

3.1 We do not sell your data.

3.2 Service Providers (Data Processors)
We share data with trusted service providers who help us operate Kinesa, such as
hosting, analytics, and crash reporting. They are contractually required to
protect your data and only use it to provide services to us.

3.3 Legal Requirements
We may disclose your data if required by law or to protect our rights and safety.

3.4 Business Transfers
If Grandpoint is acquired or merged, your data may be transferred to the new
owner. We will notify you before this happens.

3.5 With Your Consent
We may share data for other purposes with your explicit consent.

## 4. Data Retention

4.1 Active Accounts
We retain your data as long as your account is active and for a reasonable
period after to allow reactivation.

4.2 Account Deletion
If you delete your account, your data is permanently deleted within 30 days.

4.3 Legal Retention
We may retain data longer if required by law (for example, tax records).

4.4 Inactive Accounts
If you have not logged in for 2 years, we will send a warning and delete your
account after 90 days if you do not respond.

## 5. Data Security

We use industry-standard security practices including encryption in transit and
at rest, access controls, and regular security reviews. No system is 100%
secure.

## 6. Your Rights Under UK GDPR and EU GDPR

## 9. Data Collection and Use in Community Challenges

9.1 Additional Data We Collect in Challenges
When you participate in Unity Features, we collect:
- Challenge participation records (which challenges, when joined/left)
- Progress metrics shared with participants (steps, workouts, etc.)
- Rankings and leaderboard positions (if you opt-in)
- Posts, comments, and reactions in activity feeds
- Reports and moderation actions
- Display name and optional profile photo

9.2 How We Use Challenge Data
- To facilitate challenge participation and show progress
- To display rankings and leaderboards
- To enable social interactions (posts, comments)
- To moderate content and enforce community guidelines
- To improve Unity Features
- To send notifications about challenge updates

9.3 Who Sees Your Challenge Data
Other challenge participants can see:
- Your display name and avatar
- Your progress metrics (if you opt-in)
- Your ranking (if you opt-in)
- Your posts and comments (if activity feed enabled)

We may share challenge data with:
- Third-party partners for sponsored challenges (only with your explicit consent)
- Law enforcement (if required by law or to investigate violations)

We never share challenge data with third parties for marketing without your consent.

9.4 Your Choices
You can:
- Choose a display name different from your real name
- Opt-out of rankings
- Leave challenges at any time
- Delete your posts before leaving
- Disable Unity Features entirely in Settings

9.5 Data Retention for Challenges
- Active challenge data: retained while you participate
- After leaving: your progress is removed, but past posts remain unless deleted
- After account deletion: all challenge data deleted within 30 days
